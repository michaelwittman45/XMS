Think of it like a translator — it takes the high-voltage AC (alternating current) from your wall socket and turns it into low-voltage DC (direct current) that your motherboard, graphics card, storage, and other parts can safely use.

Installing a PSU – A Step-by-Step Guide

Installing a power supply unit (PSU) is one of the most important steps in your PC build. It powers every component, so a clean, stable installation ensures better airflow, safety, and long-term reliability. Follow these steps carefully for a smooth setup.

Before You Start – What You’ll Need

  • Phillips-head screwdriver – standard for PC cases.
  • Anti-static wrist strap (optional but recommended)
  • Zip ties or Velcro straps for cable management
  • Your PSU (non-, semi-, or fully modular)
  • Your PC case and motherboard ready for install

Step-by-Step Installation Process

  1. Position the Case: Lay your case flat with the open side facing up.
  2. Insert the PSU: Align the PSU into the bottom or top compartment (depending on your case), with the fan facing the appropriate direction (down for bottom-vented cases).
  3. Screw it In: Use the included screws to secure the PSU to the case’s mounting bracket.
  4. Connect Motherboard Power: Plug in the 24-pin ATX connector and 8-pin CPU power connector.
  5. Connect GPU Power: Use 6+2-pin PCIe connectors if applicable.
  6. Connect Drives: SATA power cables go to SSDs, HDDs, and other peripherals.
  7. Cable Management: Route cables through the case’s back panel, use zip ties, and secure everything neatly.

PSU Safety Tips

  • Always unplug the PSU from the wall before working inside your PC.
  • Double-check connectors before powering on—loose or wrong cables can damage parts.
  • Don’t force cables into sockets. If it doesn’t fit easily, it’s the wrong connector.
  • Check switch position: Ensure the PSU’s rear switch is OFF before plugging in.

Post-Installation Checklist

  • Double-check all connections – motherboard, CPU, GPU, SATA, and peripherals.
  • Turn on PSU switch (on the back) after everything is installed.
  • Power on your PC and check that fans spin and system boots to BIOS.
  • Test stability with basic software or benchmarks to confirm no shutdowns or power issues.

Troubleshooting PSU Issues

If your PC doesn't power on or behaves erratically after installing a PSU, consider these troubleshooting steps:

  • Check that the PSU switch is ON and that it’s properly plugged in.
  • Ensure the 24-pin and 8-pin CPU cables are firmly connected.
  • Try a paperclip test to see if the PSU fan turns on independently.
  • Make sure your PSU is sufficient for your GPU and CPU requirements.
